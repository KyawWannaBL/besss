### B) Executive talking points
- One shared enterprise platform powers customer, merchant, warehouse, finance, marketing, HR, branch, and rider workflows.
- Growth, operations, finance, and support are linked through a single shipment lifecycle.
- Revenue is driven by merchant activation, shipment volume, COD monetization, and operational efficiency.
- Platform maturity depends on auth cleanup, warehouse API stabilization, finance rollout, and analytics expansion.

### A) Core operations sequence
1. Delivery operations lifecycle
2. Customer service workflow
3. Customer service resolution journey
4. Shipment exception handling

### B) Slide-ready message
- Operations is the control center for shipment state, customer resolution, exception handling, and SLA monitoring.
- Supervisor, customer service, and delivery teams work from shared operational truth.
- Alerts and monitoring should drive escalation before SLA failure becomes customer churn.

## Warehouse View

### A) Warehouse story arc
1. Warehouse lifecycle
2. Warehouse state transitions
3. Warehouse API flow
4. Warehouse ERD
5. Warehouse scan-to-dispatch journey
6. Warehouse capacity planning
7. Branch + warehouse + last-mile handoff
8. Multi-branch transfer flow

### B) Slide-ready message
- Warehouse is not only storage; it is an operational transition engine from inbound to last-mile dispatch.
- QR scanning, movement logs, manifests, and storage records create full parcel traceability.
- Capacity planning links forecast volume to staffing, staging lanes, storage bins, and outbound readiness.

## Finance View

### A) Finance story arc
1. Finance portal model
2. Settlement and COD flow
3. Finance settlement journey
4. Finance ERD
5. Audit and compliance trail

### B) Slide-ready message
- Finance closes the loop between delivery execution and cash realization.
- COD, settlement batches, rider wallets, vouchers, and ledger entries should all trace back to shipment events.
- Auditability is critical for merchant trust and internal control.

## Marketing View

### A) Marketing story arc
1. Marketing portal model
2. Marketing attribution loop

4. Customer acquisition and retention loop
5. Merchant lifecycle management

### B) Slide-ready message
- Marketing is directly connected to merchant activation, consumer shipment lift, and zone expansion.
- Attribution must link campaign spend to lead creation, merchant activation, shipment volume, and ROI.
- Delivery-service marketing is most effective when connected to operational proof points such as COD trust, pickup reliability, and service-zone readiness.

## HR / Admin View
